# Decision Impact Statement — Provision

## The Problem

SNAP recipients lose benefits through procedural failures — missed SAR-7 forms, unreported changes, misunderstood notices, expired certifications. The system requires people under financial and emotional stress to navigate complex paperwork on strict deadlines. When they fail, they lose food assistance. This is not a user error problem. It's a system design problem.

## What Provision Changes

| Before Provision | After Provision |
|---|---|
| SAR-7 form arrives, looks like junk mail, user ignores it | App shows: "Your SAR-7 is due in 12 days. Here are the documents you need." |
| Job change happens, user doesn't know if it needs reporting | User types "I got a raise" → app says "You likely need to report this within 10 days" with citation |
| Notice arrives with legal language, user is confused | User photographs notice → app explains in plain language: "This is a termination notice. You have 90 days to request a hearing." |
| Benefits terminated, user doesn't know what to do | Recovery screen shows: call caseworker, gather documents, request fair hearing (with copy-paste letter) |

## Measurable Impact Areas

1. **Fewer missed deadlines** — Procedural deadlines are surfaced proactively, not buried in mail
2. **Better-prepared caseworker calls** — Users arrive with a call script and know what to ask
3. **Faster recovery** — Fair hearing process explained with timeline and generated letter
4. **Reduced anxiety** — Uncertainty ("do I need to report this?") becomes clarity ("you likely need to report within 10 days")

## The One-Family Story

Maria is a single parent in Los Angeles. She works part-time and receives $768/month in CalFresh benefits for her family of 3. She got a raise — $600 more per month. She doesn't know that California requires her to report this within 10 days if it exceeds her IRT (Income Reporting Threshold).

Without Provision: Maria does nothing. Three months later, the county discovers the unreported income during SAR-7 review. She's assessed an overpayment. Her benefits are reduced by 10% to recover the overpayment. She now has less food money for her children.

With Provision: Maria opens the app, types "I got a raise at work, making $600 more per month." The app tells her: "You likely need to report this within 10 days." It shows the citation (CalFresh Policy Manual §63-503.44), the caseworker phone number, and a call script. Maria calls the same day. Her benefits are adjusted properly. No overpayment. No disruption.

**The difference between these two scenarios is $768/month in food for her family.**

## Why This Is More Than a Directory

A benefits directory says: "Here is the CalFresh program. Here are the eligibility rules."

Provision says: "You specifically, with your specific situation, need to take this specific action by this specific date. Here's why (citation). Here's what to do (call script). Here's what happens if you don't (consequence)."

That's the difference between information and navigation. That's what AI makes possible.
